10.8 How many ways can four couples sit in a row such that everyone sits next to their partner?

There are two tasks here: we must arrange the couples AND we must arrange each person within the couples.

There are 4!=244!=244!=24 ways to arrange the couples.

10.9 How many ways can nine different people be seated in a circle?

Notice that, in a circle, we cannot tell who the first person in the arrangement is. Therefore the first person will not count towards our total. So, let’s place the first person in the circle.

Next, there are 888 choices of the person who will be seated to the left of the first person. (We can’t tell who the first person is, but we can tell who is to the left of so-and-so.) Then there are 777 choices of the person who will be to the left of the second person, and so on. Therefore there are 8!8!8! or 40,32040,32040,320 ways to arrange nine people in a circle.

(There are, in general, (n−1)!(n-1)!(n−1)! circular permutations of nnn objects.)
