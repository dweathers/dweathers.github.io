Intersection is meet, union is join, and (surprise!) complement is complement. A good naive guess at the element that plays the role of 000 is ∅\varnothing∅, and that guess is correct: ∅∪E=E\varnothing \cup E = E∅∪E=E for any set EEE. If all of these sets are contained in XXX, then ∅‾=X\overline{\varnothing} = X∅​=X. The fact that 000 and 111 are complements encourages us to suggest XXX as the 111-type object, and sure enough X∩E=EX \cap E = EX∩E=E for any set EEE (assuming EEE was a subset of XXX to begin with). These five identifications are how a power set becomes a Boolean algebra. (In Chapter 18, you can prove that any finite lattice [whatever that means] is a Boolean algebra, and that will also get you there.)
